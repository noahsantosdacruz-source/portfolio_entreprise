**Transition From Wood to Plastic Bricks**



**Innovation drives material shift**

**Late-1940s advances and market demand favored durable, uniform parts over hand-finished wood.**



ABS enables precision and scale

ABS plastic allowed high-precision, mass production of consistent elements at lower unit cost.



Interlocking design boosts play

Stud-and-tube connection improved stability and creativity, forming the foundation of the LEGO system.







**The Invention and Impact of the Interlocking Brick**



Design Features

Precise clutch power and interlocking studs enabled stable, versatile constructions, fostering limitless creativity in play.





Invention Timeline

Patented in 1958, the interlocking brick system marked a pivotal moment in toy design, setting the foundation for modern construction toys.





Impact on Industry

The innovation set a new standard, inspiring competitors and driving advancements in toy safety, durability, and play value.







Market Success

Propelled LEGO to global leadership, ensuring sustained intellectual property protection and a lasting competitive edge.





**Diverse LEGO Product Lines**





City

Urban-themed sets with vehicles, services, and everyday heroes encourage open-ended roleplay. Popular with younger builders and parents seeking recognizable scenes, City drives steady sales through broad accessibility and frequent seasonal releases.



Technic

Advanced builds with gears, pneumatics, and realistic functions attract engineering-minded teens and adults. Higher price points and display-worthy models bolster premium positioning while deepening brand loyalty through technical challenge and mastery.



Friends

Character-driven sets spotlight friendship, creativity, and inclusive storytelling. Designed to appeal strongly to young girls while engaging all kids, Friends expands LEGO’s audience and sustains repeat purchases through narratives and collectible worlds.
